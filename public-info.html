AML/CFT Compliance Guide

Understanding the regulatory requirements for real estate professionals in Singapore

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Countering the Financing of Terrorism (CFT) Requirements

As of July 2025, all real estate agencies in Singapore are required to comply with enhanced AML/CFT regulations set forth by the Council for Estate Agencies (CEA) in collaboration with the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS).

Key Requirements

Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

All real estate professionals must perform CDD measures when dealing with clients, including:

  • Verifying the identity of clients using reliable documents
  • Identifying beneficial owners for corporate clients
  • Understanding the nature of the client's business
  • Ongoing monitoring of business relationships

Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR)

Salespersons must file STRs when they know or have reasonable grounds to suspect that any property represents proceeds of crime, or was used or intended to be used in connection with any criminal activity.

Record Keeping

All documents obtained for CDD purposes and records of transactions must be maintained for at least 5 years from the date the business relationship ends or the transaction is completed.

Training Requirements

All registered salespersons must complete AML/CFT training within 3 months of registration and refresher training every 2 years thereafter.
